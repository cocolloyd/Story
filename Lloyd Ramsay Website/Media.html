MEDIA

Prosperous restaurant businesses and excellent cuisine were not the only factors that launched Ramsay into stardom. The other half that solidified his notoreity is his television personality.

Gordon Ramsay launched his first show called Ramsay’s Kitchen Nightmares in 2004. The format is similar to other shows Ramsay currently runs that you may be familiar with: he works to transform failing restaurants into profitable enterprises, whether that be through the cooking, menu, preparation, or so forth. Since then, he has added on numerous television programs that soon became popular in the US, which took on versions of their British counterparts. His inflamed temper and often crude remarks made him a chef to be reckoned with; one that was both revered and terrifying.

His harsh criticisms have become an iconic staple and launched him into other media platforms, such as people tweeting their “culinary masterpieces” for his judgement and show hosts featuring him as a guest appearance. If you have a favorite media outlet, chances are we have it here for you.

TELEVISION

Of course, you cannot know Gordon Ramsay without his infamous shows. From Britain to the US,
each show has their unique twist that heightens the competition and excitement in each episode.
If you know these or watch them religiously, then skip on. But if you want a preview and categorization
for each show, then here you go!

Hell's Kitchen

Well, the graphic should give you enough context as to what the show is about. A corny get up, but don’t be fooled by the intensity of the show. Set at his restaurant named Hell’s Kitchen in Hollywood, Ramsay has chefs undergo wild cooking challenges and dinner services. Each season has a new twist to keep the flame burning, and each episode has someone crushed. In its 19th Season, it is one of the longest running shows for him to date.

General: Teamwork, Intimidating, Ruthless

Ramsay's Kitchen Nightmares

The show that started it all! Ramsay visits a different, failing restaurant each episode and has one week to transform the restaurant dumps into a prospering business. After a few months, he revisits that restaurant to see how they are doing. There is the UK show and the US show depending on your fancy of what restaurants you want to see. It’s suspenseful, it’s riveting, and, well, it’s a nightmare.

General: Timebound, Business, Harsh

Masterchef

Masterchef may be one of the most widespread of Ramsay’s shows. There’s a Masterchef Junior, a UK version, a US version, an Australian version, and the list goes on and on. Any cooking level is welcome, but only if you can take the heat of Ramsay’s judging. Each contestant works individually under time to produce a culinary dish. Try to survive each round, because if you can’t make the cut, you’re out!

General: Individual, Survivor, Crude

The F Word

F is for... Family! The dynamics shift in this family centered team cooking show. You, your mom, dad, aunt, and other members have one hour to win a high-stakes cook off. Ramsay teams up with celebrities and other special guests to judge the teams. Would you trust your family to pull you through to the end? While not as rambunctious as the other shows, The F Word still has its fiery side.

General: Family-friendly, Competition, Tough

LATE NIGHT SHOWS

watch Late Night, you may know Stephen Colbert, Jimmy Fallon, Jimmy Kimmel, and many
more. These hosts will often invite Ramsay to join in on their games or interviews. They often
will capitalize on his swearing or cooking expertise. Preview some of his most famous
appearances and get a laugh out of them!

Spill Your Guts

Prepare for laughs and your gag reflex. Ramsay and Corden take on a wheel of interesting items that they must eat if they refuse to answer a hard, personal question. Things quickly get saucy in this episode!

Making Eggs with Ramsay

Think you know how to make scrambled eggs? Well, scratch everything you know. Learn the ultimate way to make eggs with two of your favorite stars.

Treadmill Quiz with Gordon Ramsay

Fitness, cooking, and questions galore! Ramsay and Corden are back at their antics again. This time, they control each other's speed on the treadmill, and things can speed up pretty fast with these juicy questions.

The Swear Jar

Well, if you know Ramsay, you know he tends to curse. A lot. Jimmy Fallon gets a kick out of this by putting Ramsay on the spot with his own swear jar.
